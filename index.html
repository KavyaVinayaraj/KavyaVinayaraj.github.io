About

I trained as an electronics engineer but moved into data fairly early. I was more interested in what the numbers were telling people than in the systems producing them, and that hasn't really changed.

Most of my experience comes from a UK engineering firm, where I owned the weekly reporting that leadership made decisions on. The data came from three systems that often disagreed, so a lot of the job was data quality work, finding out why the numbers didn’t match and fixing it before anyone relied on them. These days I’m at a neuroscience startup building Python data pipelines and end-to-end analytical projects.

Day to day I work in Power BI, SQL, and Python. I also hold a Lean Six Sigma Green Belt, which mostly shows up as an urge to fix processes nobody asked me to fix.
